Advocacy

Certification Framework for AIF Managers

In line with SEBI‚Äôs directive, IVCA, in collaboration with NISM, has helped develop a new certification framework for AIF managers. Effective May 1, 2025, the revised format includes two modules: Series XIX-D (for Category I & II AIFs) and Series XIX-E (for Category III AIFs), designed to align with practical industry needs.

Deadline Extended for NISM Certification Requirement for AIF Managers
SEBI has extended the deadline for complying with the NISM AIF Fund Manager Certification requirement by 90 days. The extension is intended to facilitate smoother implementation of the revised certification framework for AIF managers. IVCA had earlier engaged with SEBI, requesting a timeline extension in view of the updated rollout.

Market-SOC Requirement ‚Äì Exemption for Small Managers

On April 30, 2025, SEBI exempted self-certified REs with fewer than 100 clients from the Market-SOC (M-SOC) requirement. This key relief reduces the compliance burden for small managers while maintaining oversight for larger entities.

Partly Paid Units (PPUs) ‚Äì
Engagement with RBI

Followed by IVCA‚Äôs continuous engagement, the RBI has responded on the regularization of PPUs issued by AIFs:

  • An appropriate and liberal view will be taken while dealing with compounding applications related to PPU issuances made prior to March 14, 2024.
  • Regarding Late Submission Fees (LSF): Once the reporting facility for PPUs is made available on the FIRMS portal, sufficient time will be provided for AIFs to complete reporting and avoid LSF implications.
  • Members are advised to coordinate with their respective Authorised Dealer (AD) Banks for the process of filing compounding applications, once RBI notifies the same . RBI has assured that AD Banks will assist and that a liberal view will be adopted for compounding matters relating to PPUs issued prior to March 14, 2024.

Meetings:

SEBI Meeting ‚Äì Valuation Framework Harmonization with FMs
SEBI chaired a joint meeting with AMFI and AIF representatives, and has directed that the 2 industries need to harmonizing valuation methodologies as regards to illiquid and non traded listed securities and those below investment grade. Select fund managers from industry associations attended the meet and focussed working groups are working towards this.

RBI ‚Äì Foreign Exchange Department: Engagement on PPU Compounding
Discussed the compounding of Partly Paid-up Units (PPUs) with RBI, requesting:

  • Investment Manager should be treated as the applicant for compounding, with contravention amount calculated based on first-year management fees only for non-resident investors in funds where PPUs were issued.
  • The period of contravention should be limited to the first year of issuance (not multiple years), and
  • Compounding fee should be capped at INR 10 lakhs per manager across all PPU cases.
